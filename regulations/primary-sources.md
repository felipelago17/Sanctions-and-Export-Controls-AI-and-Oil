# Primary Regulations & Official Sources

Authoritative primary sources for US export controls and sanctions. These are the starting point for any compliance analysis.

---

## Export Administration Regulations (EAR) — Full Text

**URL:** <https://media.bis.gov/regulations/ear>

The complete codified text of the EAR (15 C.F.R. Parts 730–774), administered by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), US Department of Commerce. The EAR controls exports, re-exports, and in-country transfers of dual-use goods, software, and technology. Key parts include Part 734 (scope), Part 738 (CCL), Part 742 (controls for specific reasons), Part 744 (end-user/end-use controls), and Part 774 (Commerce Control List with ECCNs).

## AI Diffusion Rule — Federal Register

**URL:** <https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/15/2025-00636/framework-for-artificial-intelligence-diffusion>

The January 2025 Interim Final Rule establishing a tiered framework for exports of advanced AI model weights and associated computing infrastructure. Creates three tiers of countries with differentiated licensing requirements. Note: implementation has been paused pending further rulemaking — see [Extraterritorial Enforcement](./extraterritorial-enforcement.md).

## OFAC Homepage

**URL:** <https://ofac.treasury.gov>

The Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), US Department of the Treasury. Administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions based on US foreign policy and national security goals against targeted foreign countries, regimes, terrorists, proliferators, narcotics traffickers, and other threats. Primary portal for SDN list searches, programme information, licences, and FAQs.

---

## OFAC Sanctions Programs & Country Information

**URL:** <https://ofac.treasury.gov/sanctions-programs-and-country-information>

Consolidated index of all active OFAC sanctions programmes, including comprehensive (country-based) and list-based (targeted) programmes. Relevant energy-sector programmes include Russia-related (RUSAGG), Iran (IFSR), Venezuela (JCGC), and Sudan.

---

## DOJ National Security Division — Export Controls & Sanctions

**URL:** <https://www.justice.gov/nsd/export-control-and-sanctions>

The Department of Justice, National Security Division (NSD) coordinates federal criminal enforcement of export control and sanctions laws. Resources include enforcement priority statements, charging guidance, and major case summaries. Criminal penalties under the Export Control Reform Act (ECRA) and the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) are enforced here.
