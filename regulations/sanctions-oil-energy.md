# Sanctions Programs, Enforcement & Oil/Energy Sector

The oil, gas, and broader energy sector sits at the intersection of many of the most significant US sanctions programmes. Russia, Iran, Venezuela, and Sudan are the primary exposure jurisdictions; the Russia-related programmes have expanded most rapidly since 2022 and now include sector-specific and price-cap measures that affect tanker operators, trading firms, insurers, and financiers globally.

## OFAC SDN & Sanctions List Search

**URL:** <https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/>

The official OFAC consolidated sanctions list search tool. Searches the Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List, Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List, Foreign Sanctions Evaders (FSE) List, and other OFAC-maintained lists simultaneously. Essential for counterparty screening; note that the 50% Rule (see [OFAC UBO & 50% Rule](./ofac-ubo-50-percent-rule.md)) means list-search alone is insufficient — UBO analysis is also required.

## Russian Oil Price Cap

The G7 price cap on Russian seaborne crude (USD 60/barrel) and refined products imposes compliance obligations on all service providers in the tanker supply chain — shipowners, charterers, brokers, insurers, and financial institutions — regardless of nationality. OFAC enforces the cap through its existing Russia-related programmes. Non-compliance can result in SDN designation of vessels and operators.
