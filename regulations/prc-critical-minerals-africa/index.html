    PRC Entities & Critical Minerals — Africa (H.R. 9087)¶

    Proposed US legislation requiring annual public disclosure of Chinese entities carrying out critical-minerals mining in Africa under conditions of forced labour or environmental harm. The amendment to H.R. 9087, introduced 8 June 2026 by Rep. Miller of Ohio, reflects Congressional attention to China's role in African resource extraction and its intersection with supply-chain enforcement, sanctions, and export-control policy.

    H.R. 9087 Amendment — Annual List of PRC Mining Entities in Africa (June 2026)¶

    Source: Amendment in the Nature of a Substitute to H.R. 9087, offered by Rep. Miller of Ohio, 8 June 2026

    The amendment directs the Secretary of State to publish — at least annually for five years from enactment — a list of:

    1. Each PRC entity reasonably assessed to be carrying out mining (large-scale or artisanal/small-scale) of critical minerals, gold, or iron in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Nigeria, Guinea, Zambia, South Africa, Zimbabwe, or other African countries either:
    2. (A) using forced labour (section 307, Tariff Act of 1930, 19 U.S.C. 1307); or

    3. (B) causing environmental harm to a protected area (water contamination, soil degradation, or ecosystem/biodiversity loss).

    4. Each mine, mining zone, or concession at which such mining occurs.

    The list must be publicly available, including online, in unclassified form. The version submitted to the relevant congressional committees (Senate Foreign Relations; House Foreign Affairs) may include a classified annex.

    Definition of "PRC Entity" — Broad Affiliate Scope¶

    Category Covered
    Government/Party control Entities under ownership, control, or influence of the PRC Government, CCP, or PRC military/intelligence
    Organised under PRC law Entities subject to PRC jurisdiction, including Hong Kong and Macau
    Majority-owned/controlled Entities majority-owned, controlled, or financed by any PRC entity
    Affiliates & JVs Parents, subsidiaries, affiliates, contractors, and joint ventures in which a PRC entity holds a controlling interest

    This broad definition mirrors the affiliate-aggregation logic of the BIS Affiliates Rule (see BIS Affiliates Rule) and OFAC's 50% Rule (see OFAC UBO & 50% Rule).

    Other Key Definitions¶

    Term Definition
    Critical mineral Section 7002(a), Energy Act of 2020 (30 U.S.C. 1606(a))
    Forced labour Section 307, Tariff Act of 1930 (19 U.S.C. 1307) — the same reference used in the Uyghur Forced Labour Prevention Act
    Artisanal & small-scale mining Rudimentary, labour-intensive, frequently unregulated mining with inhumane working conditions
    Large-scale mining Capital-intensive, mechanised, industrial-scale operations by large commercial entities
    Protected area National parks, game refuges, ecosystem reserves, or other nature preserves in the country concerned
    Environmental harm Contamination of water resources (streams, rivers, lakes, wells, aquifers, soil); soil degradation or erosion; degradation of aquatic/terrestrial ecosystems or biodiversity loss

    List Development Requirements¶

    The Secretary of State must: - Draw on open-source information — press sources, academic, non-profit, and non-state research organisations, plus information from US embassies. - Consult with the Secretaries of Labor (Bureau of International Labor Affairs), Commerce, and Treasury; the Director of National Intelligence; other Federal agency heads; and foreign-government counterparts in the listed countries.

    Compliance Relevance¶

    • Supply-chain due diligence: Companies sourcing critical minerals from African operations should assess whether upstream suppliers qualify as PRC entities under this definition, including via JV structures and affiliate chains.
    • Precursor risk: Inclusion on the list could precede import restrictions (paralleling the Uyghur Forced Labour Prevention Act's rebuttable-presumption mechanism), supply-chain sanctions, or heightened lender/investor scrutiny.
    • Forced-labour cross-reference: The Tariff Act section 307 definition underpins existing CBP enforcement mechanisms; a finding under this amendment could reinforce parallel CBP action on affected mineral imports.
    • JV governance: The express inclusion of controlling-interest JVs signals that minority positions held through a PRC-controlled JV partner will be scrutinised for affiliate-scope purposes.
