    BIS Affiliates Rule & 50% Ownership Expansion¶

    ⚠ Snap-back date: 10 November 2026 — currently suspended (Phase 1)

    The BIS Affiliates Rule expansion is suspended for one year (10 Nov 2025 – 9 Nov 2026) under a US-China trade deal framework (FR 2025-19846, 90 FR 47201). The expansion will automatically reimpose on 10 Nov 2026 (Phase 2) unless BIS issues a further extension or repeal before that date. The baseline EAR Entity List and MEU List controls remain fully in effect throughout the suspension window. Compliance programmes should complete affiliate-mapping and diligence exercises before the Phase 2 date.

    The snap-back countdown and latest status updates are maintained automatically by the BIS Affiliates Rule daily monitor workflow.

    Overview¶

    The BIS Affiliates Rule mirrors OFAC's 50% Rule in the export-control context: it treats affiliates of Entity List or MEU List parties as themselves subject to licence requirements, even if the affiliate is not independently listed. This closes a significant compliance gap where controlled-party transactions were routed through unlisted subsidiaries or joint-venture vehicles.

    The rule applies to: - Entities owned 50% or more by a single Entity List / MEU List party - Entities owned 50% or more in the aggregate by two or more such parties - Both direct and indirect ownership chains

    Sullivan & Cromwell — BIS Establishes 50% Rule for Certain Restricted End-Users (Oct 2025)¶

    Phase 1 Suspension — Seed Sources (Oct – Nov 2025)¶

    The entries below reflect the one-year suspension period. They serve as the baseline corpus against which the daily monitoring workflow de-duplicates new items.

    Sidley Austin — Commerce Adopts 50% Rule for Export Controls (Oct 2025)¶

    URL: https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/10/us-commerce-department-bureau-of-industry-and-security-adopts-50-percent-rule-for-export-controls

    Analysis of the original Affiliates Rule adoption framing it as BIS's adoption of an OFAC-style 50% rule. Covers the rule's relationship to existing OFAC ownership aggregation standards and flags divergences — notably that the BIS rule applies to foreign-to-foreign transactions that touch the US supply chain, unlike OFAC's domestic focus.

    WilmerHale — Beware of Affiliates: BIS Expands Controls with 50% Ownership Rule (Oct 2025)¶

    URL: https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20251001-beware-of-affiliates-bis-expands-controls-with-50-ownership-rule-new-diligence-obligations

    Detailed client alert on both phases of the rule. Describes the two-phase structure (Phase 1 suspension, Phase 2 automatic reimposition), the new diligence obligations for exporters during the preparation window, and recommended programme updates. A primary reference for the mechanics of the snap-back.

    Skadden — BIS Suspends Affiliates Rule for One Year as Part of US-China Trade Deal (Nov 2025)¶

    Covers the trade-deal context for the suspension, Phase 1 / Phase 2 mechanics, and the automatic reimposition provision. Includes analysis of how the Busan framework and rare-earth licensing side-agreements interact with the snap-back timeline.

    Squire Patton Boggs — BIS Issues One-Year Suspension of Affiliates Rule Expansion (Nov 2025)¶

    Focus on snap-back risk and practical preparation steps. Discusses the Busan framework / rare-earth licensing provisions that formed part of the US-China package alongside the suspension, and the implications for companies whose supply chains touch listed-party ownership structures.

    Freshfields — BIS Suspends the Affiliates Rule (Nov 2025)¶

    URL: https://www.freshfields.com/en/our-thinking/blogs/a-fresh-take/bis-suspends-the-affiliates-rule-expanding-end-user-export-controls-for-one-yea-102lvi4
