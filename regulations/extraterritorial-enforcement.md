# Extraterritorial Jurisdiction & Enforcement

US export controls and sanctions have extensive extraterritorial reach through mechanisms like the Foreign Direct Product Rule (FDPR), the de minimis rule, and correspondent-banking pressure. This page collects resources on how BIS and OFAC enforce controls beyond US borders.

---

## Morrison Foerster — Managing Export Control Risks in the AI Chip Ecosystem (Feb 2026)

**URL:** <https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/260209-managing-export-control-risks-in-the-ai-chip-ecosystem>

In-depth treatment of how the FDPR applies to AI chips produced outside the US using US-origin software or equipment. Covers extraterritorial reach over non-US chip fabs, the "substantial transformation" test, and practical compliance steps for foreign chip manufacturers and distributors. Includes a discussion of third-country transshipment risks.

## BIS Enforcement — Official Enforcement Resources

**URL:** <https://media.bis.gov/enforcement>

BIS Office of Export Enforcement (OEE) official portal. Includes charging letters, settlement agreements, denial orders, and the Consolidated Screening List. Key reading for understanding how BIS exercises enforcement discretion and what aggravating/mitigating factors it considers.

## WilmerHale — AI Diffusion Rule Officially Paused; New Guidance Elevates Risk for AI-Related Exports (May 2025)

**URL:** <https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20250515-us-export-controls-on-ai-diffusion-officially-paused-but-new-guidance-elevates-risk-for-ai-related-exports>

Despite the formal pause on the AI Diffusion Rule's implementation, BIS simultaneously issued compliance guidance that raises due-diligence expectations for all AI-related exports regardless of the rule's status.
