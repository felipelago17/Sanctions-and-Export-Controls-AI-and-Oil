# AI & Advanced Technology Export Controls

Analysis and commentary on BIS controls targeting advanced computing chips, AI model weights, and related software. The regulatory landscape has evolved rapidly since the October 2022 advanced-computing rules; the sources below cover the January 2025 AI Diffusion Rule and subsequent developments.

---

## Sidley Austin — New US Export Controls on Advanced Computing & AI Model Weights (Jan 2025)

**URL:** <https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/01/new-us-export-controls-on-advanced-computing-items-and-artificial-intelligence-model-weights>

Comprehensive overview of the January 2025 Interim Final Rule. Covers the three-tier country framework, new ECCN classifications for AI model weights, the role of Data Center Validated End Users (DCVEUs), and implications for cloud providers and hyperscalers. Includes a comparison to the October 2022 and October 2023 rules.

---

## Baker McKenzie Sanctions News — BIS Interim Final Rule on AI & Advanced Computing ICs

**URL:** <https://sanctionsnews.bakermckenzie.com/bis-issues-interim-final-rule-and-call-for-comments-on-new-export-controls-on-artificial-intelligence-and-advanced-computing-integrated-circuits/>

Focus on the integrated-circuit controls component of the AI Diffusion Rule, including new ECCN 3A090 parameters and the Total Processing Performance (TPP) thresholds that determine licensing requirements. Useful for hardware manufacturers and chip designers.

---

## WilmerHale — BIS Issues Long-Awaited Export Controls on AI (Feb 2025)

**URL:** <https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/publications/20250205-bis-issues-long-awaited-export-controls-on-ai>

Analysis of the policy rationale behind the AI Diffusion Rule and its interaction with existing EAR controls. Addresses the model-weight definition, the "publicly available" exclusion, and compliance programme expectations for AI developers.

---

## National Law Review — BIS Issues Four Key Updates: Advanced Computing & AI Export Controls

**URL:** <https://natlawreview.com/article/bis-issues-four-key-updates-advanced-computing-and-ai-export-controls>

Summary of four simultaneous regulatory updates accompanying the Diffusion Rule: (1) Entity List additions, (2) Military End-User (MEU) list expansion, (3) Validated End-User (VEU) programme changes, and (4) new Foreign Direct Product Rule (FDPR) provisions targeting AI supply chains.

---

## Mayer Brown — Commerce Announces New Export Compliance Expectations for AI (May 2025)

**URL:** <https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2025/05/us-commerce-department-announces-new-export-compliance-expectations-related-to-artificial-intelligence>

Covers BIS guidance on compliance programme expectations specifically for AI-related exports: know-your-customer obligations for AI hardware distributors, due-diligence requirements for model-weight transfers, and red-flag indicators published by BIS for AI transactions.
