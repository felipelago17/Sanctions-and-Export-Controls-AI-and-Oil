# OFAC UBO Aggregation & 50% Rule

OFAC's 50% Rule is one of the most consequential and least well-understood rules in US sanctions compliance. It extends SDN-equivalent restrictions to any entity owned 50% or more (individually or in aggregate) by one or more SDN-listed parties — **even if that entity does not itself appear on the SDN List**.

> **See also:** Felipe Villasuso Lago, *"The Affiliate Trap: OFAC's 50% Rule and Aggregation Risk in Joint Ventures"*, Oxford Business Law Blog (OBLB), 2026. The article analyses how the aggregation mechanic creates hidden sanctions exposure in multi-party joint ventures and private equity structures, with particular focus on the oil & gas sector.

## OFAC FAQ — Topic 1521: The 50% Rule

**URL:** <https://ofac.treasury.gov/faqs/topic/1521>

The authoritative OFAC guidance on the 50% Rule. Explains that US persons are prohibited from dealing with entities owned 50% or more by one or more SDN-designated parties, even absent a separate SDN designation of the entity. Addresses aggregation across multiple SDN owners, indirect ownership chains, and the interaction with sectoral sanctions.

Key points from the FAQ:
- Ownership is assessed at the time of the transaction
- Aggregation applies even where no single SDN owns 50%+ alone
- The rule applies to all OFAC sanctions programmes unless a specific programme states otherwise
- OFAC does not maintain a comprehensive list of blocked entities under the 50% Rule — parties must conduct their own due diligence
